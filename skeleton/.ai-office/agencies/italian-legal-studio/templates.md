# Italian Legal Studio — Document Templates

## Overview

Pre-configured templates for common Italian legal documents. Each template includes:
- Proper Italian legal terminology
- Mandatory formatting (headers, footers, signature lines)
- Placeholder guidance for content
- Compliance checkpoints
- Citation format (Italian legal standards)

---

## General Guidelines for All Templates

### Italian Legal Language Standards

1. **Formal Register Only**
   - Use "Lei" (formal you), not "tu"
   - Avoid colloquialisms
   - Use past participles for passive voice

2. **Article Citations**
   - Format: "Codice Civile, art. 1234" or "C.c., art. 1234"
   - Full statute text on first citation, abbreviated thereafter
   - Include year for recent laws

3. **Numbers and Amounts**
   - Euro amounts: "€ 1.500,00" (period for decimals, comma for thousands in Italian format)
   - Dates: "15 marzo 2026" (not "15/03/2026")
   - Article numbers: spelled out ("articoli") in text, abbreviated (art./artt.) in citations

4. **Formatting Requirements**
   - Header with party names and identification numbers
   - Numbered sections (I., II., III. or 1., 2., 3.)
   - Signature block at end with title, date, stamp
   - Page footer with "pagina [X] di [Y]"

### Quality Assurance for Legal Documents

**Before Partner Review:**
- [ ] Spell-checked (Italian dictionary)
- [ ] All Code references verified in current edition
- [ ] Case law references current (within 5 years)
- [ ] Dates properly formatted
- [ ] Amount figures correct
- [ ] Party names consistent throughout
- [ ] Signature lines properly spaced (minimum 2cm)

**Partner Review Checklist:**
- [ ] Legal argument sound
- [ ] No obvious case law contradictions
- [ ] Client interests protected
- [ ] Opposing arguments addressed (if applicable)
- [ ] Recommendations strategic
- [ ] Compliance risks identified
- [ ] Cost/benefit analysis included (in memos/opinions)
